Direct costs

These are the costs that go directly into building your product into something sellable. It is important you know your direct costs so you don't end up selling your product for less than you paid to make it.

Record all of your direct costs in the table to calculate your total.

Overhead costs

These are the ongoing costs associated with running your whole business operation. These costs are important to know as it will help you determine how much you charge for your product in order to make a profit.

Record all of your overhead costs in the table to calculate your total.

Total costs and price

Now you have calculated your direct and overhead costs, you can calculate your final cost per unit. This can be tricky but don't worry, we will tell you what you need to do.

Number of units you want to export

The first thing you can do is record how many units of your product you want to export in the following tool.

Try to be as accurate as possible, this will help make your plan stronger.

Your final cost per unit

Your final cost per unit is how much it costs your business to create one unit of your product.

To work this out you will need to use 3 pieces of information you recorded earlier:

  • how many units of your product you want to export
  • your direct costs final total
  • your overhead costs final total

You will then be able to calculate this using the tool in the next section.
